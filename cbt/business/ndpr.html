DATA PRIVACY & NDPR COMPLIANCE STATEMENT

Nigeria Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019 — Compliance Declaration

Firmvision Technologies Ltd is committed to the responsible handling of personal data in full compliance with Nigeria's National Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019, issued by the National Information Technology Development Agency (NITDA). This statement describes how personal data is handled within the Firmvision CBT Examination Platform deployed at Babcock University High School.

3. NDPR Compliance Principles

1 Lawfulness, Fairness & Transparency

Data is collected with the knowledge of the School (Data Controller) and processed only for legitimate educational assessment purposes. Students are informed of data collection through the school's enrolment process.

2 Purpose Limitation

Personal data collected is used solely for examination administration and academic record keeping. It is not used for marketing, profiling, or any purpose incompatible with its collection purpose.

3 Data Minimisation

Only data strictly necessary for platform operation is collected. The Platform does not collect biometric data, photographs, device fingerprints, or location data.

4 Accuracy

The School's administrators are responsible for maintaining accurate student records. The Platform provides tools for administrators to update, correct, or delete student data at any time.

5 Storage Limitation

Data is retained for periods defined in Section 2 above. Automated cleanup processes remove expired data. The School retains the right to export all data before deletion.

6 Integrity & Confidentiality (Security)

Technical safeguards implemented in the CBT Platform include:

  • Passwords hashed using bcrypt (work factor ≥ 12) — never stored in plain text
  • JWT tokens with configurable expiry (default: 8 hours)
  • One-time cryptographic exam session tokens (256-bit entropy)
  • HTTPS enforced in production (SSL/TLS via Let's Encrypt or school certificate)
  • Database access restricted to application service account
  • No student data transmitted to third-party services (fully self-hosted)
  • Automated database backups with encrypted archives

7 Accountability

Firmvision maintains internal records of processing activities. All support access to the system is logged and reported to the School. This Compliance Statement is made available to the School and, upon request, to NITDA.

4. Data Subject Rights

Under NDPR, students and staff (data subjects) have the right to:

RightHow to Exercise
Access their personal dataRequest via School Administrator, who can export data from the admin panel
Correct inaccurate dataRequest via School Administrator
Delete data ("right to be forgotten")Admin panel — student deletion removes all linked exam records after required retention period
Object to processingSubmit request to School Administration, escalated to Firmvision if technical action required

5. Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach, Firmvision shall:

  1. Notify the School (Data Controller) within 72 hours of discovery;
  2. Provide a full incident report within 7 days;
  3. Cooperate with the School in notifying affected data subjects;
  4. Assist in reporting to NITDA if required by NDPR.

6. Third-Party Data Transfers

The CBT Platform is fully self-hosted on the School's premises or a dedicated cloud server in Nigeria. No student data is transmitted to external services, analytics platforms, or offshore servers. All processing occurs within the Deployment Environment.
