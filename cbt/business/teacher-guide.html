1. Logging In

Open any web browser on a device connected to the school network and navigate to:

http://[school-server-address]
(Your IT Administrator will provide the exact address — e.g. http://192.168.1.100)
  1. You will see the Login Page. Click the "Admin / Teacher" tab.
  2. Enter your Username and Password as provided by the Administrator.
  3. Click "Login". You will be taken to the Admin/Teacher Dashboard.
Tip: Bookmark the login page in your browser for quick access. If you forget your password, contact your System Administrator to reset it.

3. Creating Questions

The Question Bank is your library of reusable questions. Build it before creating exams.

3.1 Adding a New Question

Step 1. Click "Question Bank" in the sidebar.
Step 2. Click the "+ Add Question" button (top right).
Step 3. A form will appear. Fill in the details:
FieldDescription
SubjectSelect the subject this question belongs to (e.g. Mathematics, English)
Question TypeChoose: MCQ, True/False, Multi-select, or Essay (see below)
DifficultyEasy, Medium, or Hard — for your own organisation
Class Levele.g. SS1, SS2, JSS3 — filters questions when building exams
Question TextType your question clearly. Avoid ambiguity.
MarksPoints awarded for a correct answer (default: 1)
ExplanationOptional explanation shown to students after submission

3.2 Question Types Explained

Multiple Choice (MCQ): One correct answer from 2–6 options.

  • Add 2–6 answer options
  • Select the radio button next to the correct option
  • Students earn full marks for the correct choice, zero for wrong

True / False: Binary choice — automatically set to True and False options.

  • Select whether "True" or "False" is the correct answer
  • Auto-scored: full marks for correct, zero for wrong

Multi-Select: Multiple correct answers — students must select ALL correct options.

  • Add 3–6 options; check all options that are correct
  • Scoring: partial credit for partially correct answers; penalties for selecting wrong options
  • Best used for "select all that apply" type questions

Essay / Long Answer: Open text response — manually graded.

  • Provide a model answer or marking guide in the "Explanation" field
  • After exam submission, you grade essays manually from the Results page
  • Score of 0 is recorded until you enter the actual marks
Tip: Write clear, unambiguous questions. Avoid trick questions unless the learning objective specifically requires higher-order thinking. Have a colleague review questions before adding them to an exam.

4. Creating & Managing Exams

4.1 Creating a New Exam

Step 1. Click "Exams" in the sidebar → click "+ New Exam".
Step 2. Fill in the exam details:
FieldDescription
Titlee.g. "SS2 Mathematics Mid-Term Exam 2026"
SubjectSelect from the dropdown
ClassWhich class can access this exam (e.g. SS2)
Duration (mins)Total time allowed, e.g. 60 or 90
Pass Mark (%)Minimum percentage to pass, e.g. 50
Max ViolationsHow many anti-cheat violations before auto-submit (default: 3)
Start / End TimeSchedule when the exam is accessible. Leave blank for manual activation.
InstructionsSpecial instructions shown to students before starting
Step 3. Click "Save". The exam is created in Draft status — students cannot see it yet.

4.2 Adding Questions to an Exam

Step 1. On the Exams page, click the 📋 Manage Questions button next to the exam.
Step 2. The Question Manager opens with two panels:
  • Left panel: Questions currently in this exam
  • Right panel: Your question bank (filter by subject, difficulty, class)
Step 3. Click "Add" next to any question to include it. Click "Remove" to take it out.
Tip: Add more questions than you need, then use the randomisation feature to show a random subset to each student.

4.3 Activating an Exam

When you are ready for students to take the exam:

Step 1. On the Exams list, find your exam (status: Draft).
Step 2. Click "Activate". Status changes to Active.
Step 3. Students whose class matches the exam can now see and start it.
Warning: Once an exam is activated and students have started, do NOT remove questions — this may affect scoring for in-progress sessions.

4.4 Ending an Exam

When all students have finished (or the exam period is over):

  • Click "End Exam". The exam closes and no new sessions can be started.
  • Any student still in progress will be auto-submitted when the timer expires.
  • You can then view full results and grade essays.

6. Grading Essay Answers

Essay questions require manual grading. To grade:

Step 1. Go to Results → click the student's name to open their session detail.
Step 2. Essay answers appear in purple cards labelled "Essay Answer."
Step 3. Read the student's response. Enter the marks awarded in the score box (0 to max marks).
Step 4. Click "Save Grade". The student's total score updates immediately.
Tip: The question's "Explanation" field (visible to you in the session detail) can contain your marking guide or model answer for reference.

7. Tips for Writing Good CBT Questions

MCQ Best Practices

  • Write questions that test understanding, not just recall of memorised text
  • All distractors (wrong options) should be plausible — not obviously wrong
  • Avoid "all of the above" and "none of the above" — they can be gamed
  • Keep the question stem complete — a student should understand the question before reading options
  • All options should be grammatically consistent with the stem
  • Avoid negative phrasing ("Which is NOT...") unless clearly highlighted

Essay Best Practices

  • State clearly what you are asking: "Describe...", "Explain...", "Discuss..."
  • Specify the expected length or number of points: "List THREE..."
  • Prepare a model answer and marking guide before the exam
  • Be consistent in marking — grade all students on the same question back-to-back

8. Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
Can't log in — "Invalid credentials"Check username/password. Caps Lock? Contact admin to reset password.
Question bank is emptyNo questions created yet. Click "Add Question" to begin building your bank.
Student says they can't see the examCheck: (1) Is the exam Active? (2) Does the student's class match the exam's class?
Results show 0 for essay questionsNormal — essays need manual grading. Go to Results → student session → grade essays.
Student's exam was auto-submitted unexpectedlyCheck violation log in session detail. Student likely exceeded the max violations limit.
Platform not loading on student deviceEnsure the device is connected to the school LAN (Wi-Fi or cable). Try a different browser.
Timer shows incorrect timeThe timer is server-authoritative. Local device clock does not affect it. Contact IT if wildly off.
