NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

Mutual Confidentiality Agreement

This Non-Disclosure Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into as of ________________ between:

Party A: Firmvision Technologies Ltd, Ota, Ogun State, Nigeria ("Firmvision")

Party B: Babcock University High School, Ilishan-Remo, Ogun State, Nigeria ("School")

Collectively referred to as the "Parties".

1. Purpose

The Parties wish to explore and execute a business relationship concerning the development and deployment of a Computer-Based Testing (CBT) Platform. In the course of this relationship, each Party may disclose confidential information to the other. This Agreement governs the treatment of such information.

2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" means any non-public information disclosed by either Party to the other, whether orally, in writing, or in electronic form, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably should be understood to be confidential given the nature of the information and circumstances of disclosure, including but not limited to:

  1. Technical designs, source code, software architecture, algorithms, and system configurations;
  2. Business plans, pricing, financial projections, and commercial strategies;
  3. Student personal data, examination content, question banks, and academic records;
  4. Staff information, administrative credentials, and access tokens;
  5. Any information marked "Confidential", "Proprietary", or "For Internal Use Only".

3. Obligations of Receiving Party

Each Party receiving Confidential Information ("Receiving Party") agrees to:

  1. Hold the Confidential Information in strict confidence and not disclose it to any third party without prior written consent from the Disclosing Party;
  2. Use the Confidential Information solely for the purpose of the CBT project and associated services;
  3. Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or representatives who have a legitimate need to know and are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations;
  4. Notify the Disclosing Party promptly upon becoming aware of any unauthorised disclosure or use.

4. Exclusions

These obligations shall not apply to information that:

  1. Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement;
  2. Was rightfully known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure;
  3. Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;
  4. Is required to be disclosed by applicable law or a court order, provided the Disclosing Party is given reasonable prior notice.

5. Student Data (NDPR Compliance)

Both Parties acknowledge that student personal data is subject to Nigeria's National Data Protection Regulation (NDPR) 2019. Firmvision, as data processor, shall:

  1. Process student data only on documented instructions from the School (data controller);
  2. Implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect student data;
  3. Not transfer student data outside Nigeria without explicit authorisation;
  4. Promptly notify the School of any data breach.

6. Term

This Agreement shall remain in effect for a period of three (3) years from the date of signing, or for the duration of the business relationship plus two (2) years, whichever is longer. Obligations regarding student data shall survive indefinitely.

7. Return of Information

Upon request or termination of the business relationship, each Party shall promptly return or certifiably destroy all Confidential Information of the other Party and confirm such destruction in writing.

8. Remedies

The Parties acknowledge that any breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Each Party is entitled to seek injunctive relief and any other remedies available under Nigerian law.

9. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the Federal Republic of Nigeria. Any disputes shall be resolved through negotiation and, if necessary, arbitration in Lagos, Nigeria.
